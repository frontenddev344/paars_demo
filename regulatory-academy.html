What We Offer

Our Services Under Regulatory Academy

Effective regulation depends on public officials and professionals who understand how policies are developed, implemented, and improved. The Regulatory Academy offers specialized training for governments, oversight bodies, and professionals who work in or around regulatory systems.

Public sector capacity building

We design training programs that help public institutions improve regulatory design, delivery, and oversight.

  • Training for oversight bodies to support coordination, quality
    control, and transparency

  • Practical courses for ministries and regulators on core
    regulatory tools, implementation and compliance

  • Modules on smart and agile regulation, including sandboxes,
    risk-based tools, and AI in policymaking

  • Long-term institutional training programs tailored to national
    strategies

We offer specialized training for lawyers, economists, consultants, and overall professionals working on regulation or advising regulated entities.

  • Courses on regulatory governance, impact assessments,
    transparency, and accountability

  • Executive and academic programs developed in collaboration
    with universities

  • Certification options for professionals seeking expertise in
    specific areas of regulatory policy and public law

  • Training on digital and AI-driven
    regulation

  • Custom sessions for legal, compliance, and corporate affairs
    teams in emerging sectors

Professional training & academic programs

Workshops & short-term training

Our workshops offer hands-on training on key regulatory topics for governments, legal experts, academics, and private professionals with case studies, and real-world applications.

  • Introduction to Better Regulation and public
    governance

  • Impact assessment tools (e.g., RIA, CBA,
    SCM)

  • Stakeholder engagement and public
    consultation strategies

  • Transparency and accountability in decision-
    making

  • Simplification and reduction of administrative
    burdens

  • Agile regulation, AI in regulation, and
    regulatory sandboxes
